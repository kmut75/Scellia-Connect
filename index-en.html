FAQ

Your questions, answered

Everything you need to know before sending your first parcel or offering your first trip.

Yes, absolutely. Transporting goods for others in personal luggage is a common and legal practice governed by aviation regulations. Each passenger is legally responsible for the contents of their bags — which is precisely why Scellia requires content verification before any acceptance.

In practice, the traveller inspects the parcel, takes photos before closing the bag, and confirms the absence of prohibited items. This procedure is compliant with DGAC (French Civil Aviation Authority) requirements and protects both traveller and sender.

What Scellia adds: a formal structure to a practice that has always existed in the community — with traceability, a digital contract, and protection for both parties.
The escrow is the heart of the Scellia trust system. Here is how it works in practice:

1. On trip confirmation — The sender pays the agreed amount. Funds are immediately held in a secure account managed by Stripe, our PCI-DSS certified payment partner. The traveller cannot access them at this stage.

2. During transport — Funds remain locked regardless of circumstances. Neither the sender nor the traveller can access them without the other party's validation.

3. On delivery — The recipient receives a 4-digit OTP code via WhatsApp. They give it to the traveller, who enters it on the platform. This dual validation automatically triggers the bank transfer to the traveller within 2–3 business days.

In case of dispute — If delivery is contested within 48 hours, the Scellia team acts as a neutral mediator, uses photo evidence and the activity log, and resolves within 72 hours maximum.
Every shipment includes a parcel protection cover in the price. Here is what it covers:

Total loss — You are reimbursed up to the declared value at the time of shipment creation (ceiling of €500 per parcel).

Partial damage — Damaged items are compensated based on photos taken at handover and delivery. The value differential is refunded.

Significant delay — If delivery exceeds 5 days after landing without explanation, the escrow is automatically returned to the sender while the situation is resolved.

Our key advantage: every step is photographed and timestamped. In the event of a claim, you have a complete evidence file — something no informal system can offer.
The recipient is at the heart of the tracking system, without needing to install anything. Everything happens via WhatsApp and SMS — the tools they already use every day.

They receive a notification at each key step:

📦 Shipment created — Confirmation, traveller's name, expected departure date.
🛫 Flight departed — Flight number, estimated arrival time in Kinshasa.
🛬 Landing confirmed — Actual arrival time, delivery estimate.
🚗 Traveller en route — Alert 30 minutes before estimated arrival.
🔐 OTP code — 4-digit code sent via WhatsApp and SMS to validate handover.
🎉 Delivery confirmed — Final confirmation with digital receipt.

The recipient does not need a recent smartphone. The service works with any phone capable of receiving SMS.

Becoming a Scellia traveller is straightforward. Simply sign up, verify your identity (ID document), and publish your trip with your flight number, date, and available kilos.

The full process in 4 steps:

1. Sign up & identity verification — Complete profile + verified ID photo. Fast, 100% online.
2. Publish your trip — Flight number, date, available kilos, desired price per kg.
3. Automatic matching — Scellia suggests compatible requests for your route. You choose which ones to accept.
4. Transport & delivery — Hand over the parcel to the recipient in Kinshasa and enter the OTP code. Payment is transferred within 2–3 business days.

How much can you earn? A traveller carrying 15 kg on a Paris–Kinshasa trip receives approximately €171 net (15 kg × €11.44/kg after Scellia commission). Over the year, a regular traveller (4–6 trips) can generate €700–1,000 in supplementary income.
